HARRY POTTER AND THE PHILOSOPHER'S STONE

The story begins with an orphaned baby named Harry Potter left on the doorstep of his abusive Aunt Petunia and Uncle Vernon Dursley's home. On his eleventh birthday, Harry discovers that he is a wizard and receives a letter of acceptance to attend Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Harry discovers his fame as "The Boy Who Lived" and unravels the mystery of the Philosopher's Stone, an object of great power. Facing the dark forces of Voldemort, they confront Professor Quirrell, who seeks the Stone. With courage and friendship, they thwart Voldemort's plans, marking the beginning of Harry's magical adventures at Hogwarts.

HARRY POTTER AND THE CHAMBER OF SECRETS

Harry returns to Hogwarts and faces mysterious attacks that petrify students. He discovers his ability to speak Parseltongue and uncovers a hidden chamber containing a dangerous monster. With Ron and Hermione's help, they learn about Tom Riddle and his connection to the Chamber. Ginny Weasley is taken, and Harry confronts the memory-phantom of Tom Riddle. Using a basilisk fang and Gryffindor's sword, Harry defeats the phantom, saves Ginny, and frees Dobby the house-elf. The school is safe, but the fight against Voldemort continues. Harry's second year ends with newfound strength and knowledge about his enemy.

HARRY POTTER AND THE PRISONOR OF AZKABAN

"Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban," Harry's third year at Hogwarts is overshadowed by Sirius Black's escape, a supposed follower of Voldemort. Harry learns about Dementors, soul-sucking creatures guarding the school, and discovers Black's connection to his past. With the help of new teacher Professor Lupin and magical creature Buckbeak, Harry unveils the truth – Black is innocent and his godfather. In a thrilling confrontation, Harry saves Sirius from the Dementors, realizing the importance of friendship and loyalty. The year ends with a mix of relief and uncertainty as Harry's journey in the wizarding world continues.

HARRY POTTER AND THE GOBLET OF FIRE

"Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire," the fourth year at Hogwarts brings the Triwizard Tournament to the school, but Harry is underage and not eligible to participate. However, his name mysteriously emerges from the Goblet of Fire, making him a participant. Throughout the dangerous challenges, Harry must navigate a series of life-threatening tasks, while suspicions arise about the tournament's fairness. As the tournament unfolds, Harry faces unexpected betrayals, dangerous encounters, and the return of Lord Voldemort. The book culminates in a tragic event that alters the course of the wizarding world and sets the stage for the dark times to come.

HARRY POTTER AND THE ORDER OF PHOENIX

"Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix," Harry faces a tough fifth year at Hogwarts as the Ministry of Magic refuses to believe Lord Voldemort's return. He forms Dumbledore's Army to teach fellow students defensive magic. As Voldemort's influence spreads, Hogwarts undergoes changes, including the appointment of the cruel Professor Umbridge. The Order of the Phoenix, a secret organization fighting Voldemort, aids Harry. He dreams of Voldemort's actions and finds a connection between them. In the climactic battle, Voldemort tries to retrieve a prophecy concerning him and Harry, but Sirius Black dies in the struggle. The book ends with Harry vowing to continue the fight against darkness.

HARRY POTTER AND THE HALF BLOOD PRINCE

"Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince," Harry's sixth year at Hogwarts is fraught with danger as he delves deeper into Lord Voldemort's past. Dumbledore guides Harry in understanding Voldemort's weaknesses and horcruxes—objects containing fragments of his soul. Meanwhile, Draco Malfoy's mysterious task and Severus Snape's allegiance become key concerns. As romance blossoms, tragedy strikes when Dumbledore and Harry retrieve a horcrux. Snape kills Dumbledore, revealing his darker side. The book concludes with Hogwarts in chaos, leaving Harry with a heavy burden to continue his quest to destroy the horcruxes and defeat Voldemort once and for all.

HARRY POTTER AND THE DEATHLY HALLOWS

"Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows"With Hogwarts under Voldemort's control, the trio goes on the run while seeking clues left behind by Dumbledore. Their quest takes them to various locations, facing danger and sacrifice along the way. They encounter Deathly Hallows—legendary magical objects—the Elder Wand, the Resurrection Stone, and the Invisibility Cloak, which are said to make one the Master of Death.As they gather the horcruxes, the trio learns about Voldemort's dark past and the key to his defeat. Harry discovers he is the final horcrux and must die to weaken Voldemort. In a heart-wrenching twist, Harry willingly sacrifices himself to Voldemort but finds himself in a limbo-like King's Cross station, where he chooses to return to the living world to finish the battle.In the final showdown, Hogwarts is besieged by Death Eaters, and many beloved characters fall in the battle. Harry faces Voldemort and, with the true allegiance of the Elder Wand, he overpowers him. The dark lord is finally defeated, and peace is restored in the wizarding world.
